Lecture Recommendations

I have thoroughly enjoyed the following lectures and highly recommend them to anyone interested in these fields. While some are specific to the universities I have attended, others are accessible online. (And yes, I have heard multiple lectures on the same topics!)
  • Linear Algebra - Gilbert Strang's Linear Algebra lectures are essential for anyone exploring machine learning. He takes the time to develop a deep intuition for each topic, making the material incredibly engaging and insightful.
  • Topology & Geometry - This lecture series by Tadashi Tokieda is one of my absolute favorites. While introducing the field, he masterfully demonstrates the elegance of visual problem-solving. Don't forget to draw pictures!
  • Computer Vision - Andreas Geiger's publicly available lecture series is my top recommendation for Computer Vision. It provides a solid introduction to the field while also covering more advanced and contemporary topics.
  • Big Data - Ghislain Fourny's lecture at ETH Zurich is a joy to follow for anyone keen on Big Data. It thoroughly addresses the core topics of storing, retrieving, and manipulating large-scale datasets.

Publications

  • CL-Splats: Continual Learning of Gaussian Splatting with Local Optimization

    TL;DR: We explore how to adapt an existing 3DGS scene representation to new inceremental changes. We propose a novel and efficient way to identify changed regions and then to locally optimize them. This not only produces more accurate scene updates but also enables new applications.

    Under Review

    Jan Ackermann, Jonas Kulhanek, Shengqu Cai, Haofei Xu, Marc Pollefeys, Gordon Wetzstein, Leonidas Guibas, Songyou Peng

    Website

  • AIpparel: A Large Multimodal Generative Model for Digital Garments

    TL;DR: We introduce AIpparel, the first large-scale multimodal generative model designed specifically for digital garments. By extending LLaVA to incorporate a new garment modality, AIpparel enables the creation of sewing patterns from text, image, and garment inputs. Our novel tokenization scheme supports the generation of complex patterns beyond the capabilities of previous approaches, while the dedicated garment modality facilitates seamless iteration on garment designs.

    Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2025

    Kiyohiro Nakayama*, Jan Ackermann*, Timur L. Kesdogan*, Yang Zheng, Maria Korosteleva, Olga Sorkine-Hornung, Leonidas Guibas, Guandao Yang, Gordon Wetzstein

    Arxiv | Website

  • Do Efficient Transformers Really Save Computation?

    TL;DR: We explore the class of Linear and Sparse Transformers in a Chain-of-Thought (CoT) setting, finding that to match the performance of regular Transformers, their hidden dimensions must scale with the problem size. However, we also identify a simple criterion that enables efficient Transformers to operate even more effectively.

    International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2024

    Kai Yang, Jan Ackermann, Zhenyu He, Guhao Feng, Bohang Zhang, Yunzhen Feng, Qiwei Ye, Di He, Liwei Wang

    Paper | Arxiv

  • Maskomaly: Zero-shot Mask Anomaly Segmentation

    TL;DR: We show that pretrained Mask-based segmentation models can predict anomalies without further tuning. Additionally, we introduce a metric for anomaly segmentation that favors models with confident predictions.

    British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC), 2023 (oral)

    Jan Ackermann, Christos Sakaridis, Fisher Yu
