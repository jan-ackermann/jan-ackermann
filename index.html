Lecture Recommendations

I have thoroughly enjoyed the following lectures and highly recommend them to anyone interested in these fields. While some are specific to the universities I have attended, others are accessible online. (And yes, I have heard multiple lectures on the same topics!)
  • Linear Algebra - Gilbert Strang's Linear Algebra lectures are essential for anyone exploring machine learning. He takes the time to develop a deep intuition for each topic, making the material incredibly engaging and insightful.
  • Topology & Geometry - This lecture series by Tadashi Tokieda is one of my absolute favorites. While introducing the field, he masterfully demonstrates the elegance of visual problem-solving. Don't forget to draw diagrams!
  • Computer Vision - Andreas Geiger's publicly available lecture series is my top recommendation for Computer Vision. It provides a solid introduction to the field while also covering more advanced and contemporary topics.
  • Big Data - Ghislain Fourny's lecture at ETH Zurich is a joy to follow for anyone keen on Big Data. It thoroughly addresses the core topics of storing, retrieving, and manipulating large-scale datasets.
